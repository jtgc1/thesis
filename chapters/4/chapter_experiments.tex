\section{Real Data}
\label{sec:real_data}

The real measurement dataset was collected from an active biostimulation remediation site on the Canadian prairies. It consists of 27 data points drawn from 11 stake locations distributed across the contaminated area. Each stake location hosts a sensor array at three depths below the surface: 1.5~m, 2.5~m, and 3.5~m. These depths were chosen to capture the vertical extent of the PHC plume, which tends to migrate downward through the soil column under the influence of gravity and precipitation-driven leaching.

At each depth, PHC concentration is recorded continuously at 30-minute intervals. To reduce high-frequency measurement noise while preserving the underlying remediation dynamics, these raw readings are aggregated into 12-hour averages, yielding two observations per depth per day at each stake location. Each data point in the dataset therefore consists of a time series of PHC concentration measurements at three depths from a single stake location, sampled at 12-hour increments.

The duration of the time series varies considerably across data points, ranging from 30 to 250 days. This variability reflects the practical realities of field deployment: sensors were installed and occasionally decommissioned at different times throughout the remediation campaign, and some units experienced extended periods of dropout due to hardware faults or adverse weather conditions. As a result, the dataset is irregular both spatially---with stake locations distributed non-uniformly across the site---and temporally, with each location contributing a different length of observational record. These characteristics make the dataset representative of the kind of incomplete, heterogeneous data that spatial interpolation methods must contend with in real remediation settings.

Two representative samples from the dataset are shown in Figure~\ref{fig:real_data_samples}, illustrating the range of behaviours observed across stake locations. Some locations exhibit a clear monotonic decline in PHC concentration consistent with active biostimulation, while others show more complex dynamics including periods of stagnation or transient increases, likely attributable to soil heterogeneity or localized variations in nutrient distribution.


\section{Synthetic Data}
\label{sec:synthetic_data}

The synthetic data largely mirrors the real data in format. Each data point contains temporal data at three depths of 1.5, 2.5 and 3.5 meters with standard measurements every 12 hours. However, all of this will vary when testing the methods under a variety of conditions.

The synthetic data is made from a physics-based simulation that incorporates diffusion, advection and biodegradation of PHC concentration. The simulation operates in one-dimension through the depth of the soil to match the set-up of the stake measurements. The cell size is fixed to 0.1 meters deep per cell. The depth of the simulation is also fixed for all data points to 4.5 meters, allowing for a buffer between the lowest measurement and the boundary layer at the bottom of the simulation.
